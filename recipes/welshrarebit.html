Welsh Rarebit

Description

This is style of grilled cheese of welsh origin. Made by using a roux base to melt the cheese in and then spreading that sauce over some sourdough bread before baking.

Ingredients

  • 4 thick slices of Sourdough Bread
  • 8oz of Shredded White Cheddar
  • 2 tbsp of Flour
  • 2 tbsp of Butter
  • 1/3 cup of milk
  • 1 tbsp of Apple Cidar Vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of Dijon Mustard
  • 2 teaspoons of Sambal Olek
  • 1 teaspoon of Worchestershire Sauce
  • 1 egg

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Add slices of sour dough to a baking sheet.
  2. Melt butter in small pot and then stir in flour to create a roux. Allow to cook for about 1 minute, stirring, until the flour gives off a toasty aroma
  3. Combine the applecidar vinegar and milk, then add liquid to the roux. Stir until thickened then remove from heat
  4. Add in dijon, sambal olek, and worchestershire sauce. Stir to combine
  5. Mix in 2/3s of the shredded white cheddar and stir to combine. Then add in the egg and stir to combine
  6. Spread mixture onto sour dough slices, covering the entire surface up to the crust. Top slices with remaining white cheddar
  7. Bake in the oven for 10-15 minutes, then switch to high broiler if you want to add some darker color to the cheese and crisp up the top (1-2 minutes).
  8. Optionally top with a little more worchestersire sauce before serving or some pickled jalapenos for additional spice
